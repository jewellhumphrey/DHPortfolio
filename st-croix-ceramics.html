St. Croix Ceramics

                             

Abstract

This project interprets the historical boundaries of the enslaved village area and the materiality of afro-crucian life through the analysis of ceramics on Estate Little Princess Plantation in St. Croix, USVI. The Estate Little Princess plantation excavation consists of four remaining structures related to the enslaved laborers in a sugar production factory between 18th and 19th century during which Crucian enslavement was at its peak. This will be archaeologically analyzed by excavating the site with 40x40cm shovel probe tests and several 1x2 meter units that will be assessed spatially comparatively to each other. This will serve to provide an understanding of the materiality of the daily life and practices of the enslaved Africans on this site. Over the course of the next three years, the prevalence of ceramic type will be observed to achieve the goal of mapping out intra-island trade and information on retained Africanisms and daily life. This data will be used to determine where the next shovel probe tests will be dug in order to gain insight on community life, food ways, and living boundaries of the enslaved.
